Annotated Bibliography

Information Literacy (primary) · Research Genre Production (secondary)

Framing Statement

This page shows my annotated bibliography of 8 sources, including 6 peer-reviewed scholarly articles I found in the UCF library databases and 2 course readings. Each entry has a full MLA citation with a hanging indent, a summary of the source, and a connection to my own research project.

Why This Artifact Shows the Outcomes

This artifact shows Information Literacy because I had to learn how to find, evaluate, and cite real scholarly sources. At the start I thought any source I found in the UCF library was strong. I picked a Christian Century magazine article and got feedback that helped me see why a magazine article does not count as a peer-reviewed scholarly source. After that, I went back to the library, checked the “Scholarly (Peer Reviewed)” and “Full Text” boxes, and found stronger articles. This artifact also shows Research Genre Production because the annotated bibliography is its own genre, with rules I had to learn: MLA hanging indents, full citation pieces, summaries that name the topic / method / findings, and clear connections to my own project.

Feedback I Received

Dr. Barnickle flagged the Christian Century piece as a magazine article rather than a peer-reviewed scholarly journal, and explained why it would not count as a scholarly source.

How I Revised

I went back to the UCF library databases (Academic Search Premier), turned on the Scholarly (Peer Reviewed) and Full Text filters, and swapped the magazine article for a real peer-reviewed research article.
